Gameplay

In Dota 2, you'll join a team of five players and battle it out against another team to destroy their "Ancient" while defending your own. But it's not just about mindlessly attacking; each hero has a unique set of abilities that can be used to turn the tide of battle, and knowing when to use them is key to victory.

Explained: How to Play? 

The objective of the game is to destroy the opposing team's Ancient, which is located in their base.

To win the game, you must work together with your teammates to push into the enemy base, defeat their defenses, and destroy the Ancient.

You can earn gold and experience by killing enemy creeps and heroes, which allows you to level up and purchase items to make your hero stronger.

Competitive Tournaments

The biggest Dota 2 tournament is The International, which is held annually and is organized by Valve, the game's developer.

The International features the world's best Dota 2 teams and has a prize pool that is crowdfunded through in-game purchases, often reaching tens of millions of dollars.

Players and teams who perform well in Dota 2 tournaments can gain sponsorships, build a fanbase, and establish themselves as some of the best players in the world.

Attributes

Strength

Determines a hero's maximum health points (HP) and HP regeneration rate. Heroes with high Strength are generally durable and can absorb a lot of damage.

Agility

Determines a hero's attack speed, armor rating, and movement speed. Heroes with high Agility are generally nimble and deal physical damage quickly.

Intelligence

Determines a hero's maximum mana points (MP) and MP regeneration rate. Heroes with high Intelligence are generally powerful spellcasters who can deal magical damage and control the battlefield.
